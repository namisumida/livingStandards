Standards of Living

Using data from the World Bank, we compared several health metrics in the US and in some of the least developed countries in the world to better understand how standards of living can widely vary around the world.

By Nami Sumida
April 2019

LEAST DEVELOPED COUNTRIES

This is a categorization by the United Nations. Using a wide range of key economic, health, and education indicators, the Committee for Development Policy creates a list of least developed countries (LDCs), made up of "low-income countries confronting severe structural impediments to sustainable development". They tend to have economic and environmental vulnerabilities, resulting in long-term structural handicaps. (Read more on their page.)
